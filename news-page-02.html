Bookstart Bear at Head of Steam

July 25th, 2013

Bring your teddy bear to Head of Steam - Darlington Railway Museum on Wednesday 31st July from 12.30-3.30 for an afternoon of family activities. Meet Bookstart Bear, listen to some Teddy Bear stories, have your face painted, and do some teddy themed art and crafts. There's also a special teddy trail around the museum! The team from Darlington Libraries are working with the Museum for a fun afternoon for children and their families. There will be stories and rhymes at 1 pm, and you can meet Bookstart Bear between 1.45 and 2.15pm, other activities are available to drop in to during the afternoon. All activities are included in normal admission and in yearly membership passes.

Saving a Century

June 20th, 2013

Saving a Century is a photographic exhibition celebrating the work of the Victorian Society. The exhibition, on display at Head of Steam Museum from 2nd to 31st July, is curated by leading architectural historian Gavin Stamp, and uses archive photographs and material from throughout the Victorian Society's fifty years of fighting for historic buildings. 'Saving a Century' includes photos from around the country and shows examples of the best Victorian and Edwardian buildings and structures that the Society has campaigned to save, and some that have been lost. Robert Robertson diaries

June 11th, 2013

The Friends of Darlington Railway Centre and Museum have recently purchased two diaries at auction for the Head of Steam - Darlington Railway Museum. The diaries, written by Robert Robinson between 1859 and 1861, reveal details about life in Darlington in the mid 19th Century and about railway projects being undertaken in the region at that time. Robert Robinson, an engineer who worked for the Stockton and Darlington Railway, married into the Pease family and later resided at Beechwood. He owned an office close to North Road Station and worked on railway projects in North Eastern Railway region.

Curator Leona White-Hannant comments:

"Without the generosity of the Friends of the Museum we would have been unable to acquire these fascinating diaries. The detailed information about day to day life in Darlington, including what Robinson was reading and how much he was being charged for his groceries, makes interesting reading. This coupled with the information relating to the railway projects he was assisting with makes this acquisition an invaluable resource for those interested in local and railway history."

Cleveland Bridge Company in Photographs

May 2nd, 2013

To celebrate the Festival of the North East the museum will be exhibiting a selection of photographs from the Cleveland Bridge Collection that celebrate the region's bridge building heritage. The Cleveland Bridge and Engineering Company, a Darlington based business, was established in 1877 and has been involved with major projects around the world. The display will include photographs of projects undertaken in the North East, including the Middlesbrough Transporter Bridge, as well as photographs of Cleveland Bridge Company workers from the museum's photographic collection. The exhibition runs from 7th May - 9th June 2013. Normal museum entrance fee applies. For further information please visit the Festival of the North East website [external link].
